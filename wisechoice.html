Wise-Choice:

This project is aimed to reduce the struggles of the student to achieve their dream of higher studies. Once a student decides to study further than their higher secondary education, he is looking at a vast sea of numerous courses and universities. This project fulfils the lack of a guide for this journey. We believe that the best person to answer a question about a course is a student studying that course, and about a university, is a person studying at the university. Hence, this system will bridge the gap between a prospective student and a current student, helping the prospective student to make a Wise-Choice about his future. This is still an on-going project and will be completed by end of 2016.

Process 1: Empathize

At first, we needed to confirm whether the problem we felt, was a major issue for the prospective students. To get a proper and anonymous feedback, we created a survey and collected about 50 answers. The reason this method was chosen was because it gave us an anonymous feedback for a very simple answer, whether choosing a university and their preferred choice of course was a challenge among other such questions. This provided us with an estimate of the intensity of this issue and whether it should be considered as a problem that required to be fixed.

With the results we received, we were convinced that it was a big problem. Analysis of the results is shown as pictures besides this.

Process 2: Define

Next we began to conduct a more focused and detailed user study by conducting user interviews. We interviewed 3 students, 2 graduate students and 1 high school student aspiring for her undergraduate. The interviews gave us a clearer idea about the problems faced by them and what they desire. Since the graduate student had just gone through the problem and the high school student was currently going through the same, we received many valuable inputs.
